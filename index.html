Karen Heshi is an graduate of MFA in Fashion Design and Society fromParsons The New School and has an BA in Fashion Design womenswearfrom the London College of Fashion.


Her work revolves around technology since the digital world has become an integral part of everybody's everyday lives placing new higherdemands on people to perform and present flawless versions of ourselves.People are expected to appear 'appropriately perfect' for the occasion oract 'professionally perfect' within their roles, which over time has caused alot of new stress, discomfort and exhaustion in modern life.


Therefore, Karen merges the concept of performance and plays with perception in clothing aiming to make the wearer appear both professionalwhile also feeling relaxed. Tackling this concept inspired her to change thelanguage of construction within the garment, creating transformations within the fabrication and making the garment more flexible and breathable,while still allowing the wearer to look professional and sharp. There is illusion at play.
